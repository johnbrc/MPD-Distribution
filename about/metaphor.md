#	Maize Plant MetaphorIn the gospel of John, Jesus refers to his own mission using the metaphor of a seed that enters the ground and dies, in order to produce a large harvest. Grown throughout sub-Saharan Africa, the maize plant provides a similar, highly recognisable metaphor. ![Maize Plant Metaphor][metaphor].The Maize Plant Discipleship Syllabus is structured to symbolically reflect the metaphor of a maize plant. ###	Soil and rootsThe soil and roots represent the biblical, covenantal community, context and vocation of Israel.###	Maize plantThe maize plant represents the dynamic spiritual growth and multiplication of messianic community.###	Sunlight and rainfallThe sunlight and rainfall represent essential spiritual disciplines that stimulate and sustain the growth of messianic community.###	ApplicationMaize produce effectively represents *daily bread* for millions of African people. The messianic community is called, like the Messiah, to become a kind of *life-giving bread* to the peoples of the world (see John 6 & 20:21).## Further Information-	[Guidelines](guidelines.md)-	More [about](about.md) Maize Plant Discipleship[metaphor]:	images/mp-metaphor.png	height=500px align=right
