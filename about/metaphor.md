#	Maize Plant Metaphor> I tell you that unless a grain of wheat that falls to the ground dies, it stays just a grain; but if it dies, it produces a big harvest — *Jesus, John 12:24*Jesus refers to his own mission using a simple metaphor of seed entering the ground and dying, in order to produce a large harvest. Grown throughout sub-Saharan Africa, the maize plant provides a similar, highly recognisable metaphor. ![][mp-metaphor]###	Soil and rootsMetaphorically, the *roots* of the maize plant represent the biblical community of Israel, while the *soil*, in which the roots grow, represents the historical, cultural and geo-political contexts of Israel's covenant vocation — such as Egypt, Canaan, Babylon and the Roman occupation. ###	Maize plantMetaphorically, the maize plant represents the new-covenant messianic community, which grows out of the *Seed* of the Messiah, which goes into the ground and dies…###	Sunlight and rainfallThe sunlight and rainfall represent essential spiritual disciplines that stimulate and sustain the growth of messianic community.###	An ApplicationMaize produce effectively represents *daily bread* for millions of African people. The messianic community is called, like the Messiah, to become a kind of *life-giving bread* to the peoples of the world (see John 6 & 20:21).## Further Information-	[Syllabus](syllabus.md)-	More [about](about.md) Maize Plant Discipleship[mp-metaphor]:	images/mp-metaphor.png	height=600px align=right
