# About Maize Plant Discipleship###	A Practical, Relevant and Accessible Learning Resource-	Developed in consultation with missional leaders and learners in Burkina Faso, to be appropriate for use in African contexts-	Reliant simply upon facilitators willing to co-ordinate small-group study, reflection and discussion,-	With the goal of facilitating biblical learning that moves continuously outwards, drawing whole communities into patterns of scripturally-based discipleship, in living dialogue with contextual culture.###	Formulated in response to doctoral research-	Based upon analysis of qualitative data, from over seventy Burkinabé leaders and learners, from amongst five Christian groups-	Exploring significant tensions between modern, Western theological education and scripturally-based discipleship praxis-	Leading to the definition of a set of practical, relevant and accessible characteristics applicable to contextually-appropriate learning resources-	Resulting in the award of a [Doctorate of Missiology], from Fuller School of Intercultural Studies (Pasadena, CA), in 2013.###	Presently being prepared for publication-	A series of short, low-cost, modular handbooks-	Incorporating reflective learning and group discussions-	Suitable for formal and informal study-	Accessible for translation into other languages-	Available for republication under a free-at-source license-	Via [Maize Plant Discipleship website](http://maizeplantdiscipleship.wordpress.com) -	Follow progress via Twitter: [@MPDResource](http://twitter.com/mpdresource)##	Prospectus -	[Text] (prospectus.md)-	[Pdf](pdf/Prospectus.pdf) ⬇️## Further Information
