Digital publication takes place via [Releases], which package files for download, in various formats, in order to facilitate three distinct purposes:

1. [Study](#study)
1. [Print](#print) 
1. [Translate](#translate)

### Study

For facilitating local learning groups (and, or personal study; review; translation etc.)

Filetype	| Format	|  Viewer  	| 
| :-:	| ------	| ------	|  
`pdf`	| [PDF] A5 handbook	| PDF Reader	| 
`pdf`	| [PDF] A4 plain	| PDF Reader	| 
`mobi`	| [MobiPocket eBook]	| Kindle e-Reader	| 
`epub`	| [iBooks]	| iOS (iPad, iPhone)	| 
`html`	| [HTML]	| Web browser	| 

### Print

For printing inexpensive, duplex handbooks, with full- or single-colour covers. 

| Filetype	| Format	|  Detail  	| Purpose	|  
| :-:	| :--	| :-----	| :-----	|  
`pdf`	| A5 handbook	| paginated	| duplex print 	|  
`pdf`	| A4 handbook cover	| CMYK separations 	| full-colour cover	|   
 `pdf`	| A4 handbook cover	| monochrome	|single-colour cover	|   

See [contextual publication] for further information relating to printing and distribution.

### Translate

Editable, plain-text files, from which digital publications (above) using a computerised typesetting [process].

Filetype	| Format	|  Editor  	|  
| :-:	| ------	| ------	|  
`md`	| [Multimarkdown]	| Markdown-compatible	|   
`txt`	| Plain text	| Windows-compatible	|  
`tex`	| XeTex 	| [MacTex], or equivalent	|

[Releases] use shorthand to identify translations:

| Translation	|	shorthand	|  
| ------	| :----:	|  
| English	| EN	| 
| French	| FR	|  

Hence, MPD-M4-FR would be a French translation of Module 4.

See [Intercultural Republication], for further information relating to translation.
